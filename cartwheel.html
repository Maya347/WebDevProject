Cartwheel Tips

Ready to flip into something fun? Learning cartwheels is a playful and empowering way to build coordination, strength, and confidence â€” no gymnastics background needed! Whether you're working on your very first attempt or aiming to perfect your form, cartwheels are a great way to get moving, stay active, and enjoy every spin along the way.

Keep your body aligned during cartwheels

To practice your alignment:

  • Start in a lunge: Begin with your front leg bent and your back leg straight to create a strong, balanced takeoff.
  • Place your hands in a straight line: Your hands should follow the path from your front foot to where your back foot will land.
  • Finish in a lunge facing the opposite direction: A controlled ending shows proper alignment and balance.
  • Practice consistantly

Practice on a line for better precision

To increase precision:

  • Use a tape line or chalk mark: Practicing on a visual line helps train your body to stay straight.
  • Place your hands directly on the line: This teaches correct hand placement and body rotation.
  • Land with feet straddling the line: A solid finish shows control and builds consistency.

Use your arms for power and control

To increase power and control:

  • Reach high before you go down: Stretch your arms up by your ears to build momentum and stay tight.
  • Keep arms strong and straight: Locked elbows help support your weight and prevent collapsing.
  • Push through your shoulders: This keeps you lifted and helps you avoid sinking into the floor.
