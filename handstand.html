Handstand Tips

Ready to turn your world upside down? Learning handstands is an exciting way to build strength, balance, and focus — no experience required! Whether you’re aiming for a few seconds against a wall or mastering a freestanding handstand, this is your chance to challenge yourself, gain confidence, and have fun every step of the way.

Practice on the wall first

To practice on the wall:

  • Find an empty wall. It does not have to be flat. As long as you can kick up, you're good.
  • Place your hands about 8 in away from the wall and kick up.
  • Stay in this position for 3 to 5 seconds. Focus on squeezing your core muscles and using your fingers to distribute weight.
  • Practice consistantly

Strengthen Wrists and Shoulders

To increase wrist strength and flexibility:

  • Shoulder shrugs in handstand: push tall, shrug shoulders up/down
  • Box handstand holds: hands on floor, feet on box; practice straight line
  • Plank to pike lifts: build core strength for pressing up

Improve Balance

To increase balance:

  • L-shape kick-ups: practice controlled kick into partial handstand
  • Handstand holds with spotter: someone supports legs lightly
  • Freestanding holds from wall: push gently away from wall, balance
